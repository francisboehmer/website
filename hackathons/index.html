Story Camp

I was given the amazing opportunity to participate in the first annual Story Camp hackathon, hosted by The University of Waterloo School of Interaction Design and Business. Over the course of 15 hours, more than 100 other students and I formed ourselves into groups and began working to craft a compelling story.

Throughout the course of the day, we had the chance to attend workshops and work with our teams. All 15 groups were challenged to create a story which followed the theme of community and connectivity. At the beginning of the day, groups were also given the opportunity to sign up for an area of focus including visual storytelling, social media, user experience, game design or branding and business. Each group was also assigned a different story arc to follow such as The Hero’s Journey, and given a box containing various objects such as dice or a deck of cards, which we were encouraged to weave into our stories. Midway through the day, we were also surprised with a brief description of a character which we were also encouraged to incorporate into our narratives.

At the end of the long day, each team got the opportunity to pitch their story to a panel of judges using a combination of three predetermined modes. Written, Auditory or Visual (Images / Videos).

My team and I chose branding as our area of focus. We worked together to develop a social movement to help people have a sense of belonging in their community. We wrote a story to sell and idea rather than a product. Ultimately, we we’re able to complete each of the specified requirements and deliver our three-minute pitch. For our mode, we opted to do audio rather than video, since this was the route the majority of other groups took. We narrated our story and included a practical component to tie it all together.

I am honoured to say that my team came in first place. I am very proud of the work we did in such a short period of time. Story Camp challenged each and everyone one of us to work together to create something meaningful.

Storytelling is an invaluable skill which can leveraged in countless ways to grab attention, induce emotions and motivate decisions. It’s a skill that can branch into every industry around the globe. Story Camp was a unique opportunity for me to learn more about storytelling and get a chance to put it to the test.
